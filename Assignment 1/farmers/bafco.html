Thabo Mokhethi’s journey into farming started unexpectedly. After losing his job in Maseru, he returned to his rural home in Butha-Buthe and decided to plant a small plot of potatoes and beans. Using drip irrigation systems he learned about online, Thabo transformed his modest 1-hectare plot into a highly productive farm that now supplies fresh produce to roadside vendors, hotels, and supermarkets in the region.


Thabo is known for experimenting with soil enrichment techniques, like composting and crop rotation, which not only increase yields but also improve the land’s long-term fertility. His vision is to make farming attractive to youth, showing them that with the right skills and mindset, agriculture can be both profitable and sustainable.


Today, Thabo mentors young farmers and dreams of expanding his farm to include a greenhouse for year-round production.
