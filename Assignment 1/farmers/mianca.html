Mpho Tselanyane, affectionately called ‘M’e Mpho by the community, has been farming for over 12 years in the lush valleys of Leribe, Lesotho. What began as a small garden behind her home to feed her family quickly grew into a thriving business supplying maize, spinach, and carrots to local markets and schools.


She combines traditional farming wisdom passed down from her mother with modern organic techniques, ensuring her produce is both healthy and environmentally sustainable. Her biggest pride is the women’s farming cooperative she co-founded, which now mentors young girls on agricultural skills, entrepreneurship, and self-sufficiency.


Today, ‘M’e Mpho’s farm supports her family of four, creates seasonal jobs for neighbors, and provides fresh food to over 200 households in the district.
